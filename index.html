🕒 Factors that affect exposure time

  • Mesh count & color: Yellow mesh reduces light scatter, may require slightly less exposure vs white mesh.
  • Emulsion age & sensitizer: Diazo emulsions lose sensitivity over time; increase exposure by 15–20% if older than 4 weeks.
  • Lamp age & cleanliness: Dirty glass or aging bulbs extend required exposure times.
  • Relative humidity: Above 60% can increase exposure demand due to moisture in stencil.

✅ Quick verification: After exposure and washout, a properly exposed screen will have a firm stencil, sharp edges, and no pinholes. Underexposed screens break down quickly, overexposed stencils are hard to reclaim and lose detail.
